# Kyro Social Media Brand Guide

Version 1.0 — April 2026

## 1. Social Media Identity

### Who We Are on Social

Kyro is the digital loyalty platform that turns one-time visitors into regulars. On social media, we show up as the **friendly expert** — the person at the party who gives genuinely useful advice without being asked twice. We are confident but never loud. Smart but never condescending. Local at heart, modern in execution.

### Platform Positioning

| Platform | Role | Audience | Energy |
|----------|------|----------|--------|
| **Instagram** | Brand flagship — visual storytelling, product beauty, merchant spotlights | Business owners + consumers | Polished, warm, aspirational |
| **TikTok** | Discovery engine — tutorials, behind-the-scenes, quick wins | Younger business owners + consumers | Fast, honest, slightly irreverent |
| **LinkedIn** | Thought leadership — industry insights, merchant success data, company milestones | Decision-makers, B2B partners, retail/hospitality professionals | Sharp, credible, forward-looking |

### Brand Personality on Social

- **Confidently Simple** — We make loyalty feel easy, not overwhelming
- **Locally Grounded** — We celebrate real businesses, real neighbourhoods, real results
- **Effortlessly Smart** — We share insights that make people think "why didn't I know this?"
- **Quietly Ambitious** — We hint at the bigger vision without overselling

## 2. Visual System for Social

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Kyro Black** | `#0B051D` | Primary background for feed posts, text overlays, UI elements |
| **Kyro Lime** | `#E6FFA9` | Signature accent — headlines, CTAs, highlights, key stats |
| **Kyro White** | `#F9F8F5` | Body text on dark backgrounds, clean/light layouts |
| **Kyro Purple** | `#6C47FF` | Secondary accent — icons, tags, interactive elements |
| **Purple Dark** | `#2C2242` | Alternative dark background, gradient base |
| **Purple Mid** | `#AA89F2` | Tertiary accent — soft highlights, subtle UI details |

### Color Rules by Format

**Feed Posts (static)**
- Default: Kyro Black (`#0B051D`) background + Kyro Lime (`#E6FFA9`) headline + Kyro White (`#F9F8F5`) body text
- Alternate: Kyro White background + Kyro Black text + Lime accent for variety (use sparingly — max 1 in every 4 posts)
- Photography posts: Full-bleed image with Black/Lime text overlay bar at bottom or side
- Never use Lime as a full background — it is an accent, not a canvas

**Stories**
- Black background is the default canvas
- Lime for interactive elements: polls, quiz answers, swipe-up CTAs, tap highlights
- Purple (`#6C47FF`) for secondary interactive stickers and link buttons
- White for body text, always
- Keep text large — minimum 48pt equivalent for readability on mobile

**Reels / TikTok**
- Lower-third text bars: Black at 85% opacity with Lime headline text
- Full-screen text moments: Black screen + centered Lime text for impact lines
- Captions/subtitles: White text with Black stroke or Black background pill
- End card: Black background, Lime logo wordmark, White CTA

### Typography on Social

- **Headlines / Key Statements:** Klarna Title Bold — all caps or sentence case depending on energy level. All caps for punchy stats and one-liners. Sentence case for conversational headlines.
- **Body / Captions on Graphics:** Klarna Text Regular — sentence case always.
- **Fallback (when Klarna is unavailable):** Use Inter Bold for headlines and Inter Regular for body. Never use system defaults.

### Photography and Video Style

**What we shoot:**
- Real local businesses in action: baristas making coffee, hairdressers with clients, shop owners at the counter
- Wallet passes on phone screens held naturally (not staged "look at my phone" poses)
- Neighbourhood streetscapes showing the business in its context
- Hands tapping phones, scanning QR codes, receiving stamps — the moment of loyalty in action

**How we shoot it:**
- Natural light preferred; warm interior light is fine
- Slightly desaturated, warm tone — not overly filtered
- Candid over posed: capture genuine moments, not stock-photo smiles
- Shallow depth of field for product/phone close-ups
- Wide shots to establish place and vibe

**What we never shoot:**
- Empty stores or sad-looking businesses
- Overly staged "corporate handshake" moments
- Stock photography of generic people at generic desks
- Anything that looks like it belongs on a bank's Instagram

### Grid Aesthetic Strategy (Instagram)

The Instagram grid should feel like a **bold, rhythmic visual magazine** — dark, punchy, and unmistakably Kyro.

**Visual rhythm (repeating 3-column pattern):**

```
Row pattern A:  [Photo/Video]  [Bold Text Post]  [Photo/Video]
Row pattern B:  [Bold Text Post]  [Wallet Mockup]  [Photo/Video]
Row pattern C:  [Photo/Video]  [Photo/Video]  [Bold Text Post]
```

- Approximately 60% of grid tiles should have a dark (`#0B051D`) dominant tone
- Bold text posts (Lime on Black) act as visual anchors — place at least one per row
- Photography tiles should feel warm and human against the dark text tiles
- Wallet card mockups bridge the product and lifestyle content
- When viewed as a full grid, the Lime accents should create a scattered, energetic pattern — not clustered

## 3. Content Pillars

Every piece of Kyro social content falls into one of five pillars. Aim for the following weekly mix:

| Pillar | Share | Posts/Week (if posting 5x) |
|--------|-------|---------------------------|
| Product Power | 25% | 1-2 |
| Merchant Spotlight | 25% | 1-2 |
| Loyalty Intelligence | 20% | 1 |
| Consumer Side | 15% | 1 |
| Culture and Company | 15% | 1 |

### Pillar 1: Product Power

Show what Kyro does and why it is better than paper cards, old apps, or doing nothing.

**Content types:**
- Wallet card mockup carousels ("Your loyalty card, in Apple Wallet. No app needed.")
- Feature demos as reels/TikToks (setting up a card in 60 seconds)
- Before/after comparisons (paper punch card vs. Kyro digital card)
- New feature announcements
- "How it works" explainers in 3 slides

**Example post:**
> Visual: Black background, Lime text reading "Your regulars carry your card everywhere. Because it lives in their wallet."
> Caption: "No app to download. No card to lose. Your loyalty program sits right next to their boarding pass and credit card. That is the power of wallet passes. Link in bio to try it."

### Pillar 2: Merchant Spotlight (Local Hero Series)

Real stories from real businesses using Kyro. This is our equivalent of Klarna's celebrity content — except our celebrities are the cafe owner down the street.

**Content types:**
- Photo or video portrait of the merchant in their space
- Carousel: merchant quote + their Kyro card + a stat from their results
- Short-form video: "60 seconds with [Business Name]"
- Repost/share of merchants posting about their Kyro experience

**Example post:**
> Visual: Warm photo of a cafe owner behind the counter. Next slide: their Kyro card mockup. Third slide: Black background, Lime text: "42% of customers returned within 2 weeks."
> Caption: "Maria opened Southside Coffee 18 months ago. She started with Kyro on day one. Now 42% of her customers come back within two weeks. No paid ads. Just great coffee and a loyalty card that actually works. Your business could be next — link in bio."

### Pillar 3: Loyalty Intelligence

Position Kyro as the authority on customer loyalty for local businesses. Share data, insights, and non-obvious truths.

**Content types:**
- Bold stat posts (Lime on Black): "Businesses with loyalty programs see 20% more repeat visits."
- Carousel tips: "5 loyalty mistakes local businesses make"
- Industry trend commentary
- Infographics with Kyro data (anonymised and aggregated)
- Myth-busting posts

**Example post:**
> Visual: Black background, Lime text: "67% of customers say a loyalty program influences where they shop."
> Caption: "The data is clear: loyalty programs work. But only if customers actually use them. Paper cards get lost. Apps get deleted. Wallet passes stay. That is the difference."

### Pillar 4: Consumer Side

Speak directly to the people who carry Kyro passes in their wallets. Make them feel smart for using it.

**Content types:**
- "POV: You just got your 10th stamp" meme-style reels
- "Where to find Kyro" local business roundups
- Consumer tips: "How to never miss a reward again"
- User-generated content reposts (customers showing their wallet passes)
- Seasonal content: "Your holiday loyalty checklist"

**Example post:**
> Visual: Phone screen showing Apple Wallet with a Kyro pass, cozy cafe in background
> Caption: "That feeling when your free coffee is one stamp away and you actually have the card with you. Because it is literally in your phone. Tag a friend who always loses punch cards."

### Pillar 5: Culture and Company

Show the humans behind Kyro. Build trust and relatability.

**Content types:**
- Team moments (not forced, not cringe)
- Milestone celebrations ("1,000 merchants on Kyro")
- Behind-the-scenes of building features
- Values-driven content (supporting local, sustainability of digital vs. paper)
- Hiring posts
- Event recaps

**Example post:**
> Visual: Team photo at a local merchant's shop, everyone holding coffees
> Caption: "Every Friday we visit a Kyro merchant for our team coffee run. This week: @southsidecoffee. Supporting local is not just our product — it is how we work."

## 4. Caption Style Guide

### Tone

- **Clear over clever.** If a pun needs explaining, cut it.
- **Benefits over features.** Not "QR code scanning functionality" but "Customers scan, you see who came back."
- **Human over corporate.** Write like a smart friend, not a press release.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** "This works" not "We are the best loyalty platform in the world."

### Length Guidelines

| Platform | Ideal Length | Max Length |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Instagram Feed | 50-150 words | 200 words |
| Instagram Stories | 5-15 words per frame | 20 words |
| TikTok | 20-50 words | 80 words |
| LinkedIn | 100-250 words | 400 words |

### Structure

Every caption follows one of these structures:

**Hook-Explain-CTA (most common):**
> [One punchy line that stops the scroll.]
> [2-3 sentences that deliver the value.]
> [Clear CTA.]

**Story-Insight-CTA (for merchant spotlights):**
> [Start with the merchant or customer's moment.]
> [Share what happened / the result.]
> [Connect to the reader + CTA.]

**Stat-Context-CTA (for loyalty intelligence):**
> [Lead with the number.]
> [Explain why it matters.]
> [CTA.]

### Emoji Rules

- Use emojis sparingly — maximum 2-3 per caption
- Never start a caption with an emoji
- Never use emojis as bullet points (no "coffee cup, star, rocket" lists)
- Acceptable emojis: the occasional arrow for CTAs, a subtle accent that matches the content
- When in doubt, leave it out — our words do the work, not pictograms

### Hashtag Strategy

**Instagram:**
- Use 5-10 hashtags per post, placed at the end of the caption (not in a separate comment)
- Mix of brand, category, and discovery hashtags

Core brand hashtags (use on every post):
```
#Kyro #DigitalLoyalty #WalletPass
```

Rotating category hashtags (pick 3-5 per post):
```
#LoyaltyProgram #CustomerRetention #LocalBusiness #SmallBusinessTips
#AppleWallet #GoogleWallet #RepeatCustomers #CafeLife #RetailTips
#SalonBusiness #RestaurantMarketing #ShopLocal #CustomerLoyalty
#BusinessGrowth #SmallBusinessOwner
```

**TikTok:**
- 3-5 hashtags maximum
- Focus on discoverability: mix niche and trending
- Always include `#Kyro` and one broad tag like `#SmallBusinessTips`

**LinkedIn:**
- 3-5 hashtags maximum
- Professional and industry-focused: `#CustomerRetention` `#Loyalty` `#LocalBusiness` `#RetailTech` `#Kyro`

### CTA Patterns

Use varied CTAs to avoid repetition. Rotate through:

- "Link in bio to get started."
- "Try it free — link in bio."
- "Tag a business owner who needs this."
- "Save this for later."
- "Drop a [emoji] if this is you."
- "Follow for more loyalty tips."
- "DM us 'START' and we will help you set up."
- "What would you do with 40% more returning customers?"
- "Your turn — link in bio."

## 5. Platform-Specific Rules

### Instagram

**Feed Posts**
- Aspect ratio: 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait, preferred — takes more screen space)
- Resolution: 1080x1350px (4:5) or 1080x1080px (1:1)
- Carousels: 3-7 slides. First slide is the hook. Last slide is the CTA.
- Post frequency: 4-5 times per week
- Best times: Test and refine, but start with Tuesday-Thursday 11am-1pm and 7pm-9pm local time

**Stories**
- Use daily, even on non-posting days
- Mix: polls, quizzes, behind-the-scenes, merchant reposts, product tips
- Always add a location sticker for local relevance
- Use the Kyro Lime (`#E6FFA9`) as the highlight cover colour for all story highlight albums
- Story highlight albums: "How It Works" / "Merchants" / "Tips" / "About Us"

**Reels**
- Duration: 15-30 seconds (sweet spot for retention)
- Hook in first 1.5 seconds — text on screen or surprising visual
- Always add captions/subtitles
- Use trending audio when it fits naturally; never force a trend
- End with logo + CTA card (Black background, Lime logo, White CTA text)
- Post frequency: 2-3 reels per week

**Instagram Bio**
```
Kyro — Digital loyalty for local business
Stamp cards, points, rewards — in Apple & Google Wallet
No app for your customers to download
[CTA button: Get Started Free]
```

### TikTok

**Content approach:**
- Less polished, more real — but still on-brand
- Show, don't tell: screen recordings, POVs, quick demos
- Personality-forward: the person on camera should feel like a smart friend, not a spokesperson
- Participate in trends only when the trend genuinely fits the message

**Format rules:**
- Vertical 9:16 only
- 15-60 seconds (shorter wins for discovery, longer for education)
- Text on screen from second 1 — never assume sound is on
- Use Kyro Black + Lime for any on-screen text styling
- Pin top 3 videos: (1) "What is Kyro in 30 seconds" (2) best merchant story (3) highest-performing tip video

**TikTok Bio**
```
Digital loyalty cards for local businesses
Apple Wallet + Google Wallet
No app needed. Seriously.
[Link to get started]
```

### LinkedIn

**Content approach:**
- Lead with insight, not product
- Share data and results (anonymised merchant success metrics)
- Comment on industry trends with a clear Kyro perspective
- Celebrate merchant wins and team milestones
- Longer-form text posts perform well — use line breaks for readability

**Format rules:**
- Text posts: 100-250 words, short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each), generous line breaks
- Images: 1200x627px landscape or 1080x1350px portrait
- Carousels (PDF uploads): strong data storytelling, 5-10 slides
- Video: 1-3 minutes, subtitled, landscape or square
- Post frequency: 3-4 times per week

**LinkedIn Tone adjustment:**
- Same brand voice, slightly more polished
- Say "customer retention" not "getting people to come back"
- Use specific numbers: "42% repeat rate within 14 days" not "way more customers came back"
- Okay to be opinionated: "Paper loyalty cards are dead. Here is why."

## 6. Brand Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- **Do** use real photos from real merchants whenever possible
- **Do** lead with the benefit to the reader, not the feature of the product
- **Do** maintain the dark + lime visual signature — it should be instantly recognisable in a feed
- **Do** celebrate local businesses by name (with permission) and tag them
- **Do** use data and specific numbers — "42% return rate" beats "more returning customers"
- **Do** keep language simple enough that someone's parent could understand it
- **Do** show the wallet pass in context (on a phone screen, in a real moment)
- **Do** engage with comments within 4 hours during business hours
- **Do** repost merchant content that features Kyro (always credit and thank them)
- **Do** write like a person — contractions, casual punctuation, conversational rhythm

### Don't

- **Don't** use Kyro Lime (`#E6FFA9`) as a background colour — it is an accent only
- **Don't** use stock photography of people in suits shaking hands
- **Don't** write in ALL CAPS for more than 3 consecutive words
- **Don't** use jargon: no "omnichannel", no "frictionless experience", no "leverage"
- **Don't** badmouth competitors by name — focus on what makes Kyro better
- **Don't** use more than 3 emojis in a single caption
- **Don't** post the same content across all platforms without adapting format and tone
- **Don't** use the Kyro logo as a profile picture at sizes below 110x110px without testing legibility
- **Don't** create content that only makes sense if you already know what Kyro is — always assume new viewers
- **Don't** post generic motivational quotes that have nothing to do with loyalty, local business, or our product
- **Don't** use hashtags inside the body of the caption — keep them at the end
- **Don't** respond to negative comments defensively — acknowledge, offer to help, move to DM

### Visual Don'ts (with specifics)

| Don't | Do Instead |
|-------|-----------|
| Lime background with dark text | Dark background with Lime text |
| Gradients using all brand colours at once | One or two colours per composition |
| Busy layouts with 4+ elements competing | One focal point per frame |
| Tiny text on Reels/TikTok | Minimum 48pt equivalent, high contrast |
| Filters that shift the Lime to yellow or green | Preserve Lime `#E6FFA9` accuracy |
| White text on Lime background (low contrast) | Black text if Lime must be a background element |

## 7. Grid Layout Strategy

### The Kyro Grid System

Our Instagram grid is a brand asset. Every post is designed both as a standalone piece and as a tile in the larger mosaic.

### Three-Post Row Planning

Plan content in rows of three. Each row should contain:
- At least one text-dominant post (Lime on Black)
- At least one photo or video thumbnail
- A mix of content pillars

### Row Templates

**Row Type A — Product Focus**
```
[Wallet mockup on dark bg] [Lime stat text on black] [Merchant using phone]
```

**Row Type B — Merchant Story**
```
[Merchant portrait photo] [Their Kyro card mockup] [Quote/stat on black bg]
```

**Row Type C — Education**
```
[Bold tip headline on black] [Carousel cover: "5 ways..."] [Real business photo]
```

**Row Type D — Mixed Energy**
```
[Consumer POV photo] [Lime text quote on black] [Behind-the-scenes team photo]
```

### Color Balance Rules

- No two adjacent tiles should both be full Lime accent (this is unlikely since Lime is used on dark backgrounds, but monitor for "hot spots")
- No more than two adjacent photography tiles without a text/graphic tile breaking them up
- Every row of three must include at least one tile with the dark (`#0B051D`) background
- When the grid is viewed at 9-tile zoom, the overall impression should be: **dark, punctuated by warm photography and electric Lime accents**

### Pre-Publishing Checklist

Before posting, screenshot your grid preview (use a planning tool like Later, Planoly, or the native Instagram grid view) and verify:
1. The new post does not create a colour clash with its neighbours
2. The row it completes has visual variety
3. The overall grid still reads as dark, clean, and energetic at a glance
4. No two adjacent posts have the same content pillar

## 8. Engagement Voice

### How We Reply to Comments

Kyro replies like a helpful teammate, not a customer service bot and not a brand mascot.

**Tone:** Warm, specific, human. Use their name or reference their comment directly.

**Response time target:** Within 4 hours during business hours (9am-6pm local time).

**Comment types and response patterns:**

| Comment Type | Response Approach | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| Positive feedback | Thank specifically, add value | "Thanks Sarah! Glad stamp cards are working for you. Have you tried the auto-reward notification yet? Customers love it." |
| Question about product | Answer directly, link if needed | "Great question — yes, it works with both Apple and Google Wallet. Takes about 5 minutes to set up. DM us if you want a hand getting started." |
| Merchant tagging us | Celebrate them publicly | "Love seeing this! Your customers are lucky to have a spot like yours. Keep it up." |
| Negative feedback | Acknowledge, don't deflect, move to DM | "Appreciate you sharing this — that is not the experience we want you to have. DMing you now so we can sort it out." |
| Spam / irrelevant | Ignore or hide. Do not engage. | — |
| Competitor comparison | Stay focused on Kyro's strengths | "We focus on making it dead simple — wallet passes mean your customers never need to download an app. Happy to show you how it works." |

### How We Handle DMs

- Respond within 24 hours, ideally same day
- Open with their name if available
- If they are asking about the product, share a relevant link plus a one-line summary — don't make them click to get the answer
- If they are reporting an issue, acknowledge it immediately and escalate internally
- Never hard-sell in DMs. Help first, offer a free trial link naturally.

**DM template for inbound interest:**
> "Hey [Name]! Thanks for reaching out. Kyro is a digital loyalty platform — you create stamp cards, points, or rewards that live right in your customers' Apple or Google Wallet. No app for them to download. Takes about 5 minutes to set up. Want me to send you the link to try it free?"

### How We Handle Brand Mentions and Tags

- **Merchant posts featuring Kyro:** Like immediately. Comment with genuine appreciation (not generic "Thanks for the tag!"). Repost to stories within 24 hours with a thank-you overlay.
- **Consumer posts showing their wallet pass:** Like and comment. If the content is great, ask permission to repost to the main feed.
- **Press or media mentions:** Share to stories and feed with brief commentary. Tag the publication.
- **Negative mentions:** Do not ignore. Respond publicly with empathy and an offer to resolve via DM. Never argue publicly.

### Tone Calibration by Situation

| Situation | Energy Level | Example Tone |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Merchant win / success story | Warm, celebratory | "This is exactly why we built Kyro. Incredible work." |
| Product question | Helpful, direct | "Yep, works on both. Here is how to set it up." |
| Frustrated user | Empathetic, action-oriented | "Hear you. That should not have happened. Let us fix it — check your DMs." |
| Industry debate / trend | Thoughtful, opinionated | "Interesting take. Our data shows the opposite — wallet passes have a 3x higher retention than standalone apps." |
| Fun / casual engagement | Relaxed, witty (but not try-hard) | "Free coffee tastes better when you didn't have to remember a paper card to get it." |
