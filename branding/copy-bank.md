## Pillar 3: Loyalty Education

### Instagram Captions

1. It costs 5x more to get a new customer than to keep an existing one. Read that again. Then go start a loyalty program.

2. The 3 biggest loyalty program mistakes small businesses make:
   1. No program at all (your competitors have one)
   2. Paper cards that end up in the washing machine
   3. Making rewards too hard to earn
   Fix all three in 2 minutes with a digital card.

3. Loyalty members spend 67% more than non-members. That's not our stat — that's industry research. The question isn't whether you can afford a loyalty program. It's whether you can afford not to have one.

4. Quick tip: Set your reward at a frequency your customers can actually hit. If they visit once a week, a "buy 8 get 1 free" card means a reward every 2 months. That's motivating. A "buy 20 get 1 free" card? That's discouraging. Make it reachable.

5. "But my customers already come back without a loyalty program." Some of them do. But the ones who don't? You'll never know what you're missing. Data beats guessing.

6. Cafe owners: the sweet spot for a stamp card is 8-10 stamps for a free drink. Enough to feel earned, not so many that people give up. Save this for later.

7. 3 things every loyalty program needs to work:
   - Easy to join (no apps, no forms)
   - Easy to use (scan and go)
   - Rewards worth coming back for
   If any of these are broken, your program won't stick.

8. Here's a retention stat that'll keep you up at night: the average local business loses 20% of its customers every year. Not because of bad service. Because people simply forget. A loyalty program is a reminder that lives in their pocket.

9. Points vs. stamps vs. cashback — which one is right for your business? Quick guide:
   - Stamps: Best for frequent, low-cost purchases (cafes, bakeries)
   - Points: Best for variable spending (retail, restaurants)
   - Cashback: Best for high-ticket services (salons, gyms)
   Pick the one that matches how your customers buy.

10. The best time to start a loyalty program was when you opened. The second best time is today. Seriously. Every day without one is customers walking out with no reason to walk back in.

**Post 2:**
Hot take: Most loyalty programs fail because they're designed for the business, not the customer.

If your loyalty program requires downloading an app, creating an account, remembering a password, and carrying a physical card — you've already lost.

The best loyalty programs are invisible. Add to wallet. Scan at counter. Get rewarded. Done.

Friction is the enemy of retention.

**Post 3:**
A question I get asked a lot: "What reward should I offer in my loyalty program?"

Here's my framework:

1. It should feel generous (perceived value > actual cost)
2. It should be something they already buy from you
3. It should be reachable in 4-8 visits

Example: A cafe offering a free coffee after 8 stamps. The cost to the business is ~$1.50. The perceived value to the customer is $5+. And they'll spend $40+ earning it.

That's a 25:1 return. No ad platform gives you that.
