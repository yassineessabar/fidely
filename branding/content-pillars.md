# Kyro Content Strategy Framework

## Brand Quick Reference

- **Voice:** Straight Up Friendly — clear over clever, benefits over features, human over corporate
- **Personality:** Confidently Simple — friendly, smart, ambitious
- **Colors:** Black #0B051D | Lime #E6FFA9 | White #F9F8F5 | Purple #6C47FF
- **Audience:** Local business owners (cafes, salons, retail, restaurants) + consumers who use wallet passes

---

## The 5 Content Pillars

### 1. Product Showcase

**What it is:** Show the product in action. Wallet card mockups, feature walkthroughs, how-it-works explainers, UI reveals. This is where people understand what Kyro actually does — fast.

**Purpose:** Drive product awareness and convert curious followers into signups. Make the product feel tangible, modern, and dead simple.

**Post frequency:** 30% of total content

**Best formats:**
- Static mockups with phone screens
- Short reels (15-30s feature demos)
- Carousel walkthroughs (swipe to learn)
- Story polls ("Which card design do you prefer?")

**Best platforms:** Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn

**5 Post Ideas:**

1. **"Your Loyalty Card Just Got a Glow-Up"** — Side-by-side: old paper punch card vs. Kyro wallet pass on iPhone. Clean mockup, bold text overlay.
2. **"Set Up Your First Loyalty Card in 90 Seconds"** — Screen recording reel showing the full setup flow from dashboard to wallet pass.
3. **"3 Card Types. Pick Your Vibe."** — Carousel breaking down stamp cards, points cards, and cashback — when to use each, with real examples.
4. **"What Your Customer Actually Sees"** — Walk through the customer experience: tap link, add to wallet, scan at counter, earn reward. Phone screen captures.
5. **"New Feature Drop: [Feature Name]"** — Quick reveal reel with product UI, text overlays explaining the benefit, and a CTA to try it.

---

### 2. Merchant Success

**What it is:** Real stories from real businesses using Kyro. Before/after stats, testimonials, day-in-the-life content. Let merchants sell Kyro for you.

**Purpose:** Build trust and social proof. Show business owners that people like them are getting results. This is the pillar that closes deals.

**Post frequency:** 25% of total content

**Best formats:**
- Quote graphics with merchant photos
- Short interview reels (30-60s)
- Carousel case studies (the story in slides)
- Screenshot testimonials from DMs or reviews

**Best platforms:** Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook

**5 Post Ideas:**

1. **"How a Melbourne Cafe Got 400 Loyalty Members in 2 Weeks"** — Carousel case study with real numbers, photos of the cafe, and a quote from the owner.
2. **"Before Kyro vs. After Kyro"** — Split-screen graphic showing a business's repeat customer rate, average visit frequency, or revenue change.
3. **"Meet [Business Name]"** — 30s reel visiting a merchant location, showing the card in action, owner gives a quick soundbite on why they love it.
4. **"The DM That Made Our Day"** — Screenshot of a merchant message saying something genuine about results, with a short caption telling the story.
5. **"5 Local Businesses. 5 Different Industries. 1 Thing in Common."** — Carousel featuring a cafe, salon, gym, restaurant, and retail store — all using Kyro, all seeing results.

---

### 3. Loyalty Education

**What it is:** Teach your audience something useful. Stats about customer retention, tips for running a loyalty program, industry insights. Position Kyro as the expert in loyalty for local business.

**Purpose:** Attract new followers through value-first content. Build authority. Give business owners a reason to follow even before they sign up.

**Post frequency:** 20% of total content

**Best formats:**
- Stat graphics (bold number + one-liner)
- Carousel tips (5-7 slides)
- Talking-head reels with insights
- Infographics

**Best platforms:** Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok

**5 Post Ideas:**

1. **"It Costs 5x More to Get a New Customer Than to Keep One"** — Bold stat graphic with a one-line takeaway and CTA to start a loyalty program.
2. **"The 3 Loyalty Mistakes Every Small Business Makes"** — Carousel: (1) No program at all, (2) Paper cards that get lost, (3) Making rewards too hard to earn.
3. **"Why Your Regulars Stop Coming Back (And How to Fix It)"** — Reel or carousel breaking down the psychology of customer churn for local businesses.
4. **"Loyalty Program Playbook: Cafe Edition"** — Carousel with specific tips for cafes — stamp frequency, reward value, how to promote at the counter.
5. **"The Numbers Don't Lie: Loyalty Members Spend 67% More"** — Data-driven post pulling from industry research, styled with Kyro branding.

---

### 4. Behind the Brand

**What it is:** Show the humans behind Kyro. Team moments, product decisions, building in public, milestone celebrations, honest reflections. Make people root for you.

**Purpose:** Build emotional connection and brand loyalty. People buy from brands they feel connected to. This pillar makes Kyro feel real, not just another SaaS.

**Post frequency:** 15% of total content

**Best formats:**
- Selfie-style stories and reels
- Text-based LinkedIn posts
- Screenshot shares (Slack messages, metrics dashboards)
- Photo dumps from team moments

**Best platforms:** Instagram Stories, LinkedIn, TikTok

**5 Post Ideas:**

1. **"We Just Hit [Milestone] — Here's What We Learned"** — LinkedIn-style reflection post about reaching a user count, launch, or product milestone.
2. **"A Day Building Kyro"** — Story series or reel showing the team's actual workday — standup, design reviews, coffee runs, shipping features.
3. **"Why We Built Kyro (The Honest Version)"** — Founder story reel or carousel. The problem they saw, the first version, where it's going.
4. **"This Feature Almost Didn't Ship"** — Story about a product decision that was tough — what you debated, what you chose, and why.
5. **"Things We Got Wrong This Month"** — Honest post about mistakes or learnings. Builds trust and shows humility.

---

### 5. Consumer Lifestyle

**What it is:** Content for the people using Kyro wallet passes — not the merchants. The joy of earning rewards, the satisfaction of scanning, the "treat yourself" moments. Relatable, shareable, emotional.

**Purpose:** Build a consumer community around Kyro. Get end users talking about the brand so merchants see demand from the other side.

**Post frequency:** 10% of total content

**Best formats:**
- Meme-style relatable posts
- Short "POV" or "that feeling when" reels
- UGC reposts from customers
- Story interactive content (polls, quizzes)

**Best platforms:** Instagram, TikTok

**5 Post Ideas:**

1. **"That Feeling When You're One Stamp Away From a Free Coffee"** — Relatable meme or short reel with a dramatic reveal moment.
2. **"POV: You Finally Use Your Loyalty Reward"** — Quick reel showing someone scanning their wallet pass and getting a freebie, with a satisfying sound effect.
3. **"Show Us Your Wallet"** — UGC prompt asking followers to screenshot their Apple/Google Wallet with Kyro cards, repost the best ones.
4. **"Free Coffee Hits Different When You Earned It"** — Lifestyle photo/reel of someone enjoying their reward, Kyro-branded text overlay.
5. **"Things Loyal Customers Say"** — Carousel of relatable quotes: "I only go there because of the rewards," "I'm literally 2 stamps away, nobody talk to me."

## Content Batching Workflow

### Monthly Planning (1 session, ~2 hours)

1. Review last month's performance — what worked, what flopped
2. Identify the month's key theme or push (launch, seasonal, growth goal)
3. Map 20 posts across the 5 pillars using the calendar template
4. List any merchant interviews, testimonials, or UGC needed
5. Assign production tasks (design, filming, copywriting)

### Weekly Batching (1 session, ~3 hours)

1. Write all 5 captions for the week
2. Design static graphics and carousels in Figma/Canva (use Kyro templates)
3. Film and edit 2-3 reels
4. Schedule everything in your scheduling tool
5. Prep story content for the week (polls, questions, reposts)

### Daily (15 min)

1. Post stories and engage with comments/DMs
2. Monitor for UGC to repost
3. Engage with merchants and community accounts

## Seasonal and Event Hooks

### Recurring Opportunities

| Period | Hook | Content Angle |
|--------|------|---------------|
| January | New Year, New Customers | "Start your loyalty program in 2026" campaign |
| February | Valentine's Day | "Show your customers some love" — special reward ideas |
| March | End of Q1 | Merchant success roundups, Q1 stats |
| April | Easter / School Holidays | Seasonal reward ideas, family-friendly businesses |
| May | Mother's Day | Gift card + loyalty combos, salon/spa focus |
| June | End of Financial Year | "Retain more customers next FY" — education push |
| July | Mid-Year Check-In | "How's your loyalty program going?" audit content |
| August | Back to School | Student loyalty cards, cafe/food near campuses |
| September | Spring / Renewal | Refresh your loyalty card design, new season push |
| October | Halloween | Fun card designs, spooky-themed rewards |
| November | Black Friday / Small Business Saturday | "Don't just discount — build loyalty" campaign |
| December | Holiday Season | Gift cards, holiday rewards, year-in-review |

### Event-Based Hooks

- **Product launches:** 1-week content burst (teaser, launch, demo, testimonial, recap)
- **Merchant milestones:** When a merchant hits 100/500/1000 members, celebrate publicly
- **Industry news:** Customer retention studies, wallet technology updates, local business trends
- **Cultural moments:** Small Business Week, National Coffee Day, Barista Day — anything relevant to local merchants

## Content Performance Tracking

### Key Metrics by Pillar

| Pillar | Primary Metric | Secondary Metric |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Product Showcase | Saves + Link Clicks | Reach |
| Merchant Success | Shares + DMs Received | Engagement Rate |
| Loyalty Education | Saves + Follows | Reach |
| Behind the Brand | Comments + Story Replies | Engagement Rate |
| Consumer Lifestyle | Shares + Reach | UGC Generated |

### Monthly Review Questions

1. Which pillar drove the most signups or website visits?
2. Which post got the highest saves? (Saves = people found it valuable)
3. Which reel got the most shares? (Shares = people found it relatable)
4. Did any merchant content lead to inbound DMs or leads?
5. What should we do more of? Less of?
